Welcome to the Purdue Lunabotics Club!

Purdue Lunabotics is Purdue's high-achieving organization aimed at developing innovative robots for space exploration.

NASA�s Sixth Annual Robotic Mining Competition

Each year, NASA hosts a mining competition designed to mimic the hazards of extra-terrestrial mining operations. The autonomous robots must traverse Lunar or Martian soil, excavate the regolith (Lunar Dirt), and return back to base. The pioneering success of an achieving robot has the potential to become a major part of future NASA missions. Purdue Lunabotics will compete this year in May, in hopes of bringing an impact to the nation's space exploration future!

To learn more about NASA's Mining Competition in this upcoming May 2015, visit NASA Robotic Mining Competition for more details. To learn more about the rules and regulations for the competition, you can visit Robotic Mining Competition Rules.
