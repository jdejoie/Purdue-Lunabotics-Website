NASA Robotic Mining Competition 2nd Place!

  • This year, Purdue Lunabotics competed at NASA's 6th annual Robotic Mining Competition for the first time. On our competition run, our team came in 2nd place for the on-site mining section! Boiler Up!
  • Our hard work and tenacity for achieving the highest NASA standards are shown every day that we worked on the robot. The next step in this competition? 1st place.

What is NASA RMC?

  • College-level students building an automonous robot that can deposit regolith within an alotted time as a test for NASA's space exploration operations.
  • College-level students build an autonomous robot that can deposit regolith within an allotted time.
  • The unique properties of the regolith, and the reduced gravity make excavation an extremely difficult challenge.
  • The creative designs students develop may have a future impact on real-world Martian or Lunar excavation!
  • Advances in mining engineering and concepts will significantly contribute to NASA's space exploration operations.
